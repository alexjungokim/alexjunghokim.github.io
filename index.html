Alex Kim

ML Research Engineer at Amazon (AWS)

Seattle, WA

I am a Machine Learning Research Engineer at Amazon AWS, where I work on spatial and visual reasoning with Vision-Language Models. My work involves designing large-scale data engines for VLM training, scaling model pre-training across multi-node H100 clusters, and optimizing chain-of-thought inference for dynamic scene understanding.

Previously, I was a Machine Learning Researcher at Apple, where I developed cross-modal search relevancy models, text-guided image generation pipelines using latent diffusion, and RLHF evaluation frameworks for generative model alignment. My work resulted in three first-author publications and two pending patents.

My research interests span vision-language models, chain-of-thought reasoning, RLHF and alignment, latent diffusion models, and multi-object tracking. I hold an M.S. in Computer Science (Artificial Intelligence) and a B.S. in Computer Science from the University of Southern California.
